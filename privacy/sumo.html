Sumo — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 17, 2026

This privacy policy applies to the Sumo app for iOS, developed by Guillermo Rodríguez Delgado.

Data collection

Sumo does not collect personal data. There are no accounts, no ads and no tracking. All game progress stays on your device (and in your personal iCloud account if you enable device backups, which are managed entirely by Apple).

Crash reporting

To keep the app stable, Sumo uses Firebase Crashlytics (a Google service) to receive anonymous crash reports and diagnostic information when the app malfunctions. These reports describe the state of the app at the time of a crash — they do not include names, contact details, game content or anything typed into the app, and they are not used for advertising or tracking. Crash data is used solely to diagnose and fix problems.

Third-party services

Apart from Firebase Crashlytics (crash reporting only, described above), Sumo does not use third-party analytics, advertising frameworks or tracking tools of any kind.

Data sharing

No personal data is collected, so none is sold or shared. Anonymous crash reports are processed by Google Firebase on my behalf, subject to Google's Firebase privacy documentation.

Children's privacy

Sumo is designed for children and takes their privacy seriously: it does not collect personal data from anyone, including children under the age of 13. There are no accounts, no social features, no ads and no third-party tracking.

Changes to this policy

If the app's data practices ever change, this policy will be updated here before the change takes effect, and the "Last updated" date above will be revised.

Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy, contact me at gelemias@gmail.com or visit the Sumo support page.
